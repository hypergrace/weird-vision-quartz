[[Austen Osman Spare]]'s definition from [[The Book of Pleasure]]: 

> “Magic, the reduction of properties to simplicity, making them transmutable to utilise them afresh by direction, without capitalisation, bearing fruit many times”

For [[Gilbert Simondon]], the primitive magical unity is "is the relation of the vital connection between man and the world, defining a universe that is at once subjective and objective prior to any distinction between the object and the subject, and consequently prior to any appearance of the separate object."
