## Summary

This chapter deals with the tricky, fickle agreement of Whitehead's [[metaphysics]] with the general principles of [[physics]]. We deal at first w. Whitehead's primary commitment to developing a [[speculative philosophy]] schema which does not break nature ("that which we are aware of in perception") into 'primary' (substance, form, supposedly "inherent" to a sensed object) and 'secondary' (color, taste, supposedly "imparted" by the sensing subject) qualities. (e.g. [[the bifurcation of nature]])..

That is to say, physics would tell me how vibrations reach and resonate my eardrums, initiating a psychoacoustic process called hearing, but physics may not find it relevant that this particular hearing/sounding unfolds sweetly, and it certainly would not admit that sweetness is an essential reagent of the event. Then we bring in cosmology, in particular the function of Whitehead's God, which I'm curious about.
