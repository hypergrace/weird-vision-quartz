The authors abstract:  My aim here is to introduce the philosophers Deleuze-Guattari  to readers perhaps unfamiliar with their work and indicate something curious about their work, which is that it appears to have some sort of relation in a practical sense to the concept of the sorcerer. Whilst not a central figure in Deleuze and Guattari’s work, the sorcerer and the witch are themes that do crop up in their texts more often than might be expected and play more than a simply ‘metaphorical’ role. I think that [[Deleuze and Guattari]] can provide a resource for those interested in [[sorcery]], magic and [[witchcraft]] in two ways: firstly they can provide theoretical tools which can challenge or at least complement [[structuralist]], [[constructivist]] and historicist accounts and so can be of use to researchers attempting to understand these phenomena; secondly, they can provide a theoretical resource for those within the magical community who at times attempt to theorise their practise with what are essentially philosophical concepts."

## Becoming 101

[[Deleuze and Guattari]]'s concept of [[Becoming]] is a central topic to Lee's understanding of magical practices. Lee gets us into a the concept of becoming begins with a distinction between [[series and structure]]. Appropriately this comes from [[Deleuze and Guattari]]'s tenth chapter from [[A Thousand Plateau's]] [[1730: Becoming-Intense, Becoming-Animal, Becoming-Imperceptible]] between how resemblance and difference generates different formal relationships in [[series and structure]].  [[series]] is a mode by which 'a resembles b, which resembles c, etc.' where as [[structure]] relates 'a is to b as c is to d'. This distinction is synonymous with [[proportion and proportionality]]. The author suggests this is helpful to understand differences in magical practices. A series view would place magic as an antecedent to science, whereas a structure would see them as working in tandem. 

This distinction sets up further important distinctions: [[Becoming]] is not a metaphor, it is not symbolic, it is metaphysically, affectively, psychically, and virtually **real**.  

Series and structure ignore [[the anomalous]], spurred by Deleuze and Guattari's question: "is still room for something else, something more secret, more subterranean: the sorcerer and becomings (expressed in tales instead of myths or rites)?”. [[The sorcerer]] works as [[anomalogy]] and a [[conceptual personae]]. 

Lee shares an anecdote about shamanic and spiritual practices in which people develop more attuned relationships with animals. The author shares a shaman's account of a practitioner moving in a mode typically associated with a bear. The Deleuzoguattarian sentiment is that this is a 'becoming-bear', where is the shaman insists that she 'is a bear'. There are questions born out here about the nature of reality as it depends on a distinction between [[transcendence]] and [[imminence]]. This is an important distinction, because for Deleuze and Guattari: “[[Becoming]] is never imitating”, but it is also not identical [[Being]] (e.g. person = bear). Becoming is fundamental relation (e.g. person ⇌ bear). 

The author describes Deleuze and Guattari's concepts of [[blocks of becoming]], a "perfectly real" process which "produce no end result", a block of becoming produces itself. Becoming is not an evolution, but an involution, and thus a creative process. Becoming is not on an imaginary or spiritual plane but on the plane of [[imminence]].  

## Multiplicity and the Pack

The author comments on the multiplicity of self through discussed by Deleuze and Guattari in an HP Lovecraft story; "The ability to access this mode of multiplicity is what is meant by sorcery." 

"“If the writer is a sorcerer, it is because writing is a becoming”". 

Lee discusses a pack and the contradictions generated between the individual and the multiple. The anomaly is an exceptional individual, however the individual is not necessarily a single person. Packs are everywhere; a pack of cards or cigarettes. Number comes to mind, but let us return to it later. We are speaking of [[multitude]], which is to say our package packs the most heat when we become innumerable. Multitude's fertile fever.   

The anomaly is a moment when an aberrant vector breaks loose, not in orgiastic fervor, but in the quiet intensity of a gaze held a moment too long. Peace is in an intensification, a conjuring of a centrifugal "feelings of an unknown Nature." The conjuring requires the incredible power of multiplicity and contagion. It is unknown due to its real novelty. 

The anomalous animal from the pack pertains to both the alpha wolf and the mosquito in the swarm. The anomaly creates a border; this is significant because the multiplicity itself churns via contagion moving across borders. [[Alliance]] is important here; it is the relation that the multitude has with the anomalous. With the alpha wolf, a treacherous path is revealed leading towards the state. Indeed this points to the mobility or meta-stability of the pack. Its borders are constantly shifting and changing, the sites of becoming. Alliance, the craft of sorcery, mediates across borderlines without reducing them to this and that. 

## Sigilization, Difference, and Becoming

The author turns to a particular practitioner of [[magic]], in particular [[chaos magic]], called [[Austen Osman Spare]]. Lee points out Spare's objections to the use of [[symbolization]] in [[Alister Crowley]]'s form of magic.  Lee continues to describe the symbol, or symbolism, which has a relation to something else. He puts this in contrast with [[sigilization]]. A symbol is "an adjunct of something else" which has or relays its meaning. 

Sigil signal. Feel tank. For Spare, elaborated in [[The Book of Pleasure]], [[sigilization]] does not stand for or transmit. Does not convey. "Active symbolization". 

In the background of Spare's magical practice is a metaphysics involving [[Zos]] and [[Kia]], where Kia is a force similar to [[Nietzsche]]'s [[Will to Power]]. Magic is concerned with exercising and manifesting more flexibility into Kia. Spare: “Anterior to Heaven and Earth, in its aspect that transcends these, but not intelligence, it may be regarded as the primordial sexual principle, the idea of pleasure in self-love”. Perhaps also Whiteheadian [[creativity]], Lee compares to Deleuze's [[difference]] which implies a pantheism. 

For Lee, sigilization is then a technique of becoming. This is not a question then of [[belief]], directly. However, following Nietzsche, the author writes that the struggle against belief is to end in nihilism. For Spare, magic is a technique which enables the becoming of belief. Sigilization is a ritual practice by which we invite intuitive "sub-conscious or pre-conscious desire to manifest", to be possessed by desire.
