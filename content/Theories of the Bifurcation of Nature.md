Transmission doctrines (light and sound) as disrupting this vie of perception in science and philosophy: "light and sound as being something proceeding from emitting bodies" where light is connected with color by [[Isaac Newton]].

Light is waves or particles. Colors are seen. We do not perceive what enters the eye. Colors are what is seen. 

[[Locke]] has this idea of primary and second qualities, where color is a secondary quality. Secondary qualities are supposedly "not there". The consequence of this view is that we nature is incoherent and cannot be accounted for because of "the mind". "The modern account of nature is not, as it should be, merely an account of what the mind knows of nature; but it is also confused with an account of what nature does to the mind." (29). 

percipience: perceptiveness. 

"any metaphysical interpretation is an illegitimate importation into the philosophy of natural science. By a metaphysical interpretation I mean any discussion of the how (beyond nature) and of the why (beyond nature) of thought and sense awareness. **In the philosophy of science we seek the general notions which apply to nature, namely, to what we are aware of in perception.** (28)"

"For natural philosophy everything perceived is in nature. We may not pick and choose. For us the red glow of the sunset should be as much part of nature as are the molecules and electric waves by which men of science would explain the phenom,enon. It is for natural philosophy to analyse how these various elements of nature are connected." (29)

No psychic additions! "The theory of psychic additions would treat the greenness as a psychic addition furnished by the perceiving mind, and would leave to nature merely the molecules and the radiant energy which influence the mind towards that perception."

Bifurcation of nature is not a Cartesian Dualism!

"One realiy would be the entities such as electrons which are the study of speculative physics. This would be the reality which is there for knowledge ; although on this theory it is never known. For what is known is the other sort of reality, which is the byplay of the mind. Thus there would be two natures, one is the conjecture and the other is the dream."

"Now I assume as an axiom that science is not a fairy tale."

"The characters which science discerns in nature are subtle characters, not obvious at first sight. They are relations of relations and characters of characters." (pg 40)

"The fire is burning and we see a red coal. This is explained in science by radiant energy from the coal entering our eyes. But in seeking for such an explanation we are not asking what are the sort of occurrences which are fitted to cause a mind to see red. The chain of causation is entirely different. The mind is cut out altogether. The real question is, When red is found in nature, what else is found there also?"

Sense Discrimination 
"Perceptions of push are just as much the outcome of transmission as are perceptions of colour. When colour is perceived the nerves of the body are excited in one way and transmit their message towards the brain, and when push is perceived other nerves of the body are excited in another way and transmit their message towards the brain. The message of the one set is not the conveyance of colour, and the message of the other set is not the conveyance of push." (pg 44)
