## Vector Quantities

1. **Definition**: A vector quantity has both **magnitude** and **direction**.
2. **Examples**:
    - **Displacement** (e.g., 10 m east)
    - **Velocity** (e.g., 5 m/s to the north)
    - **Acceleration** (e.g., 9.8 m/s² downward)
    - **Force** (e.g., 20 N at 30° above the horizontal)
3. **Representation**:
    - Mathematically, vectors are often written in **component form**, such as v=(vx,vy,vz)\mathbf{v} = (v_x, v_y, v_z)v=(vx​,vy​,vz​) in three dimensions.
    - Alternatively, they can be represented in **magnitude–direction form**, for instance, ∥v∥=5 m/s\|\mathbf{v}\| = 5 \text{ m/s}∥v∥=5 m/s at 30∘30^\circ30∘ above the x-axis.
4. **Physical Interpretation**: Vectors answer “how much” _and_ “which way.” The direction is a crucial part of understanding the physical situation (e.g., it matters whether you push something north or south).
