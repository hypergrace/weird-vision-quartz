## Scalar Quantities

1. **Definition**: A scalar quantity is characterized only by its **magnitude** (a numerical value) and _no direction_.
2. **Examples**:
    - **Mass** (e.g., 5 kg)
    - **Temperature** (e.g., 300 K)
    - **Time** (e.g., 10 s)
    - **Energy** (e.g., 50 J)
3. **Representation**: Mathematically, a scalar can be represented by a single real number. For instance, T=300T = 300T=300 K or m=5m = 5m=5 kg.
4. **Physical Interpretation**: Scalars can be thought of as “how much” or “how many,” answering a question of quantity without specifying any direction in space.
